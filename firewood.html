Hardwood Mix

What's in Our Firewood?

Oak

Dense hardwood that burns hot and long. Creates excellent coals for sustained heat. The king of firewood.

Maple

Burns evenly with good heat output. Produces a pleasant, subtle aroma. Great for indoor fireplaces.

Ash

Lights easily and burns clean with low smoke. Excellent for fire pits and outdoor fires. Splits beautifully.

Why Seasoned Wood Matters

Green (freshly cut) wood contains 50% or more moisture. This makes it hard to light, produces excessive smoke, and creates creosote buildup in your chimney—a fire hazard. Our seasoned wood has less than 20% moisture content, ensuring safe, efficient, enjoyable fires.

Pro Tips

Getting the Best Burn

Store firewood off the ground (use a pallet or rack) and cover the top to protect from rain. Keep the sides open for air circulation. Stack in a sunny location if possible.

Seasoned wood has visible cracks on the ends, sounds hollow when knocked together, and feels lighter than green wood. The bark should pull away easily. When in doubt, use a moisture meter—look for readings below 20%.

Start with crumpled newspaper or fire starters, add small kindling, then build up to larger pieces. Make sure your damper is open. The top-down method (larger logs on bottom, kindling on top) can also work well and produces less smoke.
